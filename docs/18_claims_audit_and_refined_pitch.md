## 1. What the winners study says a 2026 winner looks like

From `materials/PHIT Winners Study - What Actually Wins (2019-2025).pdf` (our own fact-checked study):

1. **Theme-lock is absolute.** No winner has ever beaten the year's theme. 2023–2025 winners are all
   "AI systems with a *named beneficiary population*": fish farmers, smallholders, the hearing-impaired.
   The 2026 register is therefore: **a person, excluded from a capability, included by AI — visible in
   the demo itself.**
2. **Feasibility (40%) beats innovation (30%).** Half the winners pre-existed the event. Bring the
   working thing.
3. **The exportable, diplomatic story matters.** India recurs in 4/7 editions; multinational teams are
   scored. Taiwan-as-lab → New Southbound is the correct *register* for the expansion section.
4. **Winning ≠ surviving.** Every winner with an afterlife had an institution behind it. MOENV/TIFI/
   MIRDC integration is the sustainability plan, not decoration.

**Implication:** the pitch must be *one person's excluded capability, restored by AI, demonstrated live* —
not a compliance product, not a policy critique, not three modules.

## 3. The refined frame: one problem, not three

> **A 3,000-person factory can see itself. A 30-person factory cannot.**
> Corporations bought self-sight over twenty years — ERP, MES, sustainability teams — and yield,
> energy and carbon became somebody's weekly KPI. The small factory has the same data, sitting in its
> drawer as paper: bills, invoices, production logs. What it lacks is not data, or diligence, or
> intelligence — it is the **instrumentation to turn its own paper into sight**. That is the digital
> divide of the AI era: not connectivity, but **organizational self-knowledge**.
> CarbonPass restores it from a photograph — offline, on a laptop, in Taiwanese, and the raw documents
> never leave the building.

Everything we built is one product under this frame: **four blind spots, one data spine.**

| Blind spot (the owner's question) | Module (built) | The measured number |
|---|---|---|
| "What carbon is in my product?" — the buyer's letter he cannot answer | CBAM Data-Pack Engine | Commission's own example reproduced at 1e-9; pack in minutes; honest €4/t (and the stainless truth) |
| "What do I lose between buying and shipping?" — the pile he sells but cannot read | Waste Map + monthly drift alert | 1.10:1 ratio; net NT$4–5M/yr; 359 t recoverable @5% scenario; 80× the scheduler |
| "When should my machines run?" — flat operation against a moving grid | Grid-aware scheduler | NT$399,800/yr measured, live feed |
| "Am I normal?" — no benchmark exists for his sector | Anonymized cluster yield benchmark (give-back) | First of its kind; the reduction multiplier across ~1,800 firms |

**CBAM's role in the story (resolves the "assist not saviour" tension):** CBAM is not the villain and we
don't need it to be. It is the **forcing function**: the first document Europe asks for happens to be one
only an instrumented factory can produce. Answering it **instruments the factory** — and the same
instrumentation then lights everything else. The buyer's letter pays for the flashlight; the flashlight
lights the whole room.

**The AI claim (Handbook field 2.2):** not replacing labour — supplying absent capability. VLM turns
paper into the database the SME never had; rules engine reads the 2,400-page regulation so no one has to;
optimizer + drift detection think about the factory the way a corporate analyst would. All local.

## 4. The reduction theory (the part the user demanded, made defensible)

Principle: **information-driven abatement** — the same mechanism that made yield a corporate KPI.
You can only cut what you can see; we make seeing free. Levers in honest order:

1. **Yield** (structural): each point of loss ≈ 1% of precursor emissions & steel spend, 1:1 into the
   declared SEE. Range-based claim, drift alert turns annual blindness into a weekly control loop.
2. **Precursor choice / mill EPD** (supply chain): EPD unlocks ~€60/t for the buyer (firm_c measured);
   route-(E) EAF rod is 2–5× cleaner in the EU's own tables — the tool quantifies the switch per firm.
3. **Process energy** (heat treatment = the big load): benchmark kWh/t across the cluster exposes
   outliers; today no firm can know it is an outlier.
4. **Load shifting**: real money (measured), small carbon — ranked last, honestly, by our own tool.
5. **Cluster effect**: the anonymized benchmark is the multiplier — 1,800 firms discovering what
   "normal" is. This is how sight becomes sector-level reduction without a single mandate.

Trust is a feature: the tool states gross vs net, SEE vs atmosphere, and can answer **"don't bother
this year"** (Thailand row). A tool that can say "no" is a compliance instrument, not a sales brochure.
