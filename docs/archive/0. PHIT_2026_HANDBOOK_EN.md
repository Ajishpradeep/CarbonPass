## **I. Background** 

### **(I) Origin of the Event** 

The Presidential Hackathon, initiated by the Office of the President in 2019, is now in its 8<sup>th</sup> edition. This event invites individuals from diverse backgrounds, irrespective of age or nationality, to leverage open data, technology, and creative thinking in proposing compelling solutions for public issues that impact global and national development. It aims to cultivate opportunities for international, interdisciplinary, and public-private collaborations, facilitating the exchange of ideas among data owners, data scientists, and experts across different domains. Participants worldwide are encouraged to propose innovative datadriven solutions aimed at improving public welfare and fostering governmental innovation. 

### **(II) Theme Explanation** 

The Presidential Hackathon International Track invites global citizens from around the world to participate. The theme for the 2026 competition, “Digital Inclusion in the AI Era,” echoes President Lai’s New Year vision of “advancing toward a smart and prosperous Taiwan” while aligning with the growing momentum of AI development. The competition seeks innovative solutions that leverage digital technologies and tools to achieve the following objectives: (1) Promoting Digital Inclusion: Bringing AI and digital technologies closer to everyone by enabling people of different ages, regions, and needs to access technology and participate in digital services on equal footing, thereby narrowing the digital divide and ensuring all can share in the benefits of the intelligent era; (2) Building Smarter Services: Harnessing AI, data analytics, and digital tools to enhance the efficiency,

convenience, and safety of public services and everyday living environments, addressing diverse needs across education, care services, governance, disaster prevention, and beyond, while shaping a smarter and more responsive future for society. 

## **II. Eligibility and Procedures** 

- (I) Anyone can apply. We encourage participation from partners across academia, industry, civil society, government, media, and other sectors, and interdisciplinary teams are welcome. Individuals who are passionate about public and social issues are invited to participate and leverage AI, open data, and other digital technologies to propose innovative solutions that improve quality of life and advance social well-being. 

- (II) Each team must consist of 3 to 10 members, with each individual limited to joining one team only. Two members must be designated as the primary and secondary contacts. 

- (III) At least one team member must hold nationality other than that of the Republic of China (Taiwan). 

- (IV) Teams must complete their registration during the application period from May 22, 2026, to 5:00 PM (GMT +08:00) on July 31, 2026, by completing the online application form on the official event website (https://gov.tw/cYQ). All project content and related documents must be submitted and presented in English.
